# WoTMUD — PvP & the PK Glossary

Source: https://wotmud.info/pk-glossary/ (fully server-rendered)

Fetched: 2026-06-18

WoTMUD is a **PK-heavy, faction-vs-faction MUD** (Light side vs. Dark side vs. Seanchan).
The PK glossary renders in full and is the single best window into how WoTMUD's PvP
*actually plays*: it is mostly **chase, block, bait, and choke-point control** across a
known geography, with very little time spent in actual blows ("for every hour pking, you'd
be lucky to spend 10 minutes actually fighting"). The glossary is split into **General**
tactical shorthand plus four **regional location dictionaries** (Mainland, Northern,
Seanchan, Cairhien) naming the named rooms, doors, and smobs that anchor fights.

---

## 1. General PK shorthand (the tactical vocabulary)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **afk** | Away from keyboard; unlikely to respond. |
| **bloodies** | Bloody tracks — left when an enemy is **on foot** and in a **beaten/critical** condition, e.g. "bloodies heading n". A wound-state tracking signal. |
| **bricked** | Finished the **stab** timer but the stab **failed** ("you clumsily approach someone with your dagger"). See *clumsied*. |
| **clumsied** | Same as *bricked* — a failed stab after the timer completed. |
| **freshies** | Fresh tracks, e.g. "on 2 freshies heading north of appletree". |
| **lots / tons** | Saw many more enemies than expected — time to rethink strategy. |
| **lots, out** | Time to get safe and re-think. |
| **off** | "I'm not fighting anymore" — the enemy fled **off**; the fight has moved from where you narrated "on". |
| **on** | **The most basic PK signal** — the fight is on; usually needs a **location** ("on" alone is the most common mistake). Most of PK is chasing/blocking/baiting between "on" calls. |
| **out** | Get out and get safe (your side is losing). "OUT" = get out now; "OUT OUT OUT" = run like hell. |
| **pat / patty** | A **patrol** of mobs — one leader + lesser mobs (e.g. a fade + 4 ramshorned trollocs); may be stationary or mobile. |
| **pk** | Player-killing (a.k.a. PvP). |
| **ron** | Same as "on" but typed "na ron" instead of "nar on" (a narrate-typo idiom). |

**The headline insight:** the core verbs of WoTMUD PvP are **narrate-coordinate** ("on"
with a location), **track** (freshies/bloodies), **block/choke**, **bait**, and **flee to
safety** ("out"). Damage exchange is the minority of play. The whole vocabulary is built
around **shared real-time spatial awareness across a group** over a narrate channel.

---

## 2. Tracking & flee mechanics (implied by the vocabulary)

- **Tracks** are a first-class world feature: foot enemies leave **fresh tracks**
  (freshies) and, when **beaten/critical**, **bloody tracks** (bloodies). Players follow
  track trails by direction ("freshies heading north"). Live forum pastes confirm the room
  text: *"There are some bloody traces of a human leaving vvSOUTHvv"*.
- **Flee gives a (usually) random/limited direction** — choke-point rooms are valued
  precisely because **"only one flee is good"** or "you have to get a south flee to get
  out". Block spots exploit which flee directions lead to safety vs. back into the trap.
- **Horses matter** — many spots are noted as where mobs can "smell horses" at a zone edge,
  or where horses load. Mounted vs. on-foot changes the chase (and bloodies only appear on
  foot).
- **Doors** are tactical: "spammable doors" (can be opened/passed repeatedly fast) make a
  room a refuge or a trap; "fadeable rooms" host a fade (Myrddraal) patrol for mob support.

## 3. Regional location glossaries (geography as the PK board)

The bulk of the glossary is **named rooms, doors, chokes, and smobs** — proof that WoTMUD
PvP is played on a **memorized fixed map** where specific rooms have tactical identities.
Representative entries (not exhaustive — the source lists ~110 locations across 4 regions):

### 3.1 Mainland (Andor / Caralain Grass / Braem)

| Term | What it is |
|------|------------|
| **4k** | Four Kings, town just west of Caemlyn. |
| **apple / appletree** | A horseload in Caralain Grass, east of Black Hills. |
| **bgs / bridgeguards** | The eastern & western bridgeguards guarding Tar Valon entrances; provide mob help. |
| **junction (junc)** | The junction in 4k between the Caemlyn and Lugard roads — a key route node. |
| **rollie** | The **Roland smob** — a route, rest spot, and **trap** with many doors and secrets. |
| **tunnels** | Braem tunnels — **9 exits**, "a raider's best friend." |
| **wb fade** | Whitebridge fade — underground rooms, strong mob support, 2 spammable entrances; entrance "8w 8n 3e 1n of whitebridge". |
| **waygate / ways** | The Waygate in southeastern Caralain Grass. |

### 3.2 Northern (the Blight / Tarwin's Gap)

| Term | What it is |
|------|------------|
| **df choke** | Dark-forest choke (e/w-only, 2 ancient trees) — must pass to go dark forest ↔ forested borderlands; best blocks are 1e/1w of it. |
| **gap** | "Outside the Gap" (otg) and "1n gap" — Tarwin's Gap reference rooms; common fight sites. |
| **granlin** | The **Granlin / Aes Sedai / Warder smob** (in Arafel): "7w 5n from stoutdoor"; smobbed by trollocs for **jeweled cuffs**. |
| **mt** | Master Torturer — a room with a **secret door** (the smob entrance) used to block north movement. |
| **fence / pot** | Spammable-door refuge with trolloc-helping mob patrols; classic regroup/trap. |
| **orch ent** | Orchard entrance — 4-exit blighted-tree room, a bad spot if blocked. |

### 3.3 Seanchan (Toman Head / Tanchico / Falme)

| Term | What it is |
|------|------------|
| **THR** | Toman Head Road — the only road out of Falme. |
| **narrow** | N/W/E chokepoint between Seanchan safety (Falme) and human territory. |
| **oathie** | "Oathbreaker" — the Seanchan nickname for humans. |
| **LF** | Living Forest — plant-mob area with a "deathtrap"; best gone around. |
| **EF / wb** | Emond's Field / wagonbridge (1w of EF) — note "wb" is context-dependent (also Whitebridge). |

### 3.4 Cairhien (Spine of the World / Scorpion zone)

| Term | What it is |
|------|------------|
| **n spine / s spine** | North/South Spine of the World relative to Cairhien. |
| **jimmy zone** | Area named for **Jimmy the Lizard King** smob; far edge of a sane chase. |
| **mouth** | Key chokepoint — 1 north flee, 2 south, 1 nowhere; best place to block fleeing trollocs north. |
| **fg / grove** | Forgotten Grove behind 2 doors — "if a fight breaks out, one side is usually completely eliminated" (hard to exit the doors). |
| **wastelands / wastes** | High move-point-cost, trolloc-friendly mobs in nearly every room. |
| **KD** | Kinslayer's Dagger mountain range east of Cairhien. |

## 4. What the PK model teaches AnotherMUD

1. **PvP is spatial, not just numeric.** The decisive skill is **map/route knowledge and
   choke control**, not raw stats. Block spots exist because **flee direction is
   constrained** and only some exits lead to safety. Our `world-rooms-movement` flee and
   `visibility`/hidden-exit work are the substrate; a real PK layer would need **track
   trails**, **flee-direction tactics**, and **named-room legibility**.
2. **Tracks are a mechanic, not flavor.** Fresh vs. **bloody** tracks (gated on a foot +
   beaten/critical condition) turn the **condition bands** from `progression-and-exp.md`
   into a tracking signal. This couples combat-state to world-state.
3. **Mob patrols ("patties") are PvP furniture.** A leader + lesser mobs, stationary or
   mobile, that one side uses for support and the other baits — PvE and PvP are fused.
   Smobs (`crafting-and-survival.md`) double as PK landmarks and traps.
4. **Group coordination rides a narrate channel** with a terse shared vocabulary
   ("on <loc>", "out", "lots"). The social layer (our `chat`/`channel`) is load-bearing
   for PvP, not decorative.
5. **Doors (spammable / fadeable / secret) are tactical primitives** — a refuge, a trap, or
   a mob-support anchor. This maps directly onto our door/lock + hidden-exit systems.
