# WoTMUD — Game History

Source: https://wotmud.info/wheel-of-time-game-history/ (fully server-rendered)

Fetched: 2026-06-18

The site's own history of WoTMUD — "the oldest MUD in its genre." A short page, captured
near-verbatim below. Useful to AnotherMUD as context for *why* the WoTMUD mechanics look
the way they do (a 30-year-old DikuMUD-lineage codebase, PK-and-community-driven).

---

## Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| **1993** | WoTMUD created "by a bunch of ordinary gamers." The **oldest MUD set in the Wheel of Time universe**. |
| Early–mid 1990s | Genre leader since the mid-1990s. Over its lifetime **3 million+ characters created**. Early days: "hugely excited if we had more than 3 people online." |
| **1996** | **First major expansion.** Website relaunch + **launch of clans** → chosen as Yahoo's *New Site of the Day* and *Mud of the Month* by the MUD Connector. Player count went from ~10–20/night to **50–75/night**. |
| **2000** | **Second major expansion.** Another website relaunch → Netscape Recommended Site, featured in online newsletters. |
| 2000–2004 | Playerbase grew beyond 100–150; **peak of 227 simultaneous players in 2003**. Average 140–160/night, busiest Sunday & Monday evenings. |
| **2005** | **Third major expansion phase**, triggered by a **"C" → "C++" codebase change**. |

## Origins (the MUME exodus)

- WoTMUD began when friends who played a MUD called **MUME** decided to build their own.
- **Core creative team:** Flash, Zun, Mournblade, Ingtar, Kilvarnan, Mogrash, Nass — the
  first zones and coding were done by these "ex-mumers."
- A MUD called **Sanctuary** beat WoTMUD to the Wheel-of-Time theme but folded early.
- A contemporary, **Moment in Tyme**, converted from generic fantasy to Wheel of Time in
  the mid-1990s; it was successful as generic fantasy but "failed to sustain its playerbase
  when it became strictly roleplay orientated."
- Old senior immortals (e.g. Doyn, Vivienne) date from the 1996 expansion period.

## Philosophy (self-described)

- **100% free to play** — "We are not here to get your money, we just want you to have a
  good time." Explicitly positioned *against* high-budget graphical MMOs.
- **Community-driven** — "WoTmud just listens to what you personally have to say." Credits a
  thriving community and "always optimistic" mood for its longevity.
- **Maintainability over scale** — "Size isn't always an advantage."

## Why this matters for AnotherMUD

- WoTMUD is a **DikuMUD-lineage** game (its mechanics — tics, descriptive HP bands,
  spell-point pools, choke-point PK — are classic Diku re-skinned with WoT vocabulary; see
  `channeling.md`, `progression-and-exp.md`, `pvp-and-pk.md`). That lineage explains the
  shape of every mechanic we're studying.
- Its longevity (1993 → present, 30+ years, 3M+ characters) makes it the **most
  battle-tested prior art** for a real-time WoT MUD — the design choices have survived
  decades of live PK play, which is exactly the durability signal worth borrowing for our
  WoT EPIC.
- The **clans (1996) → expansion** correlation underscores that **faction/clan structure
  and PK were the growth engine**, not content volume — a useful prioritization signal.
