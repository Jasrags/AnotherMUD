# Backgrounds

Reference extracted from *The Wheel of Time Roleplaying Game* (Chapter 2: Backgrounds, pp. 22–43) for use in WheelMUD implementation.

## Overview

A character's **background** is the region or culture they arise from. A character has exactly **one** homeland — multiple backgrounds cannot be combined.

There are **eleven human backgrounds** plus a separate **Ogier** entry (a different species, not just a culture):

- Aiel
- Atha'an Miere (Sea Folk)
- Borderlander
- Cairhienin
- Domani
- Ebou Dari
- Illianer
- Midlander
- Tairen
- Tar Valoner
- Taraboner
- Ogier (nonhuman)

Background influences class fit (e.g., Aiel suits *algai'd'siswai*) but does not strictly determine class.

## What a Background Provides

Per Table 2-1, every background grants:

1. **Background Feats** — special feats unique to (or shared by) that background. Pick one at 1st level. More can be acquired later via normal feat slots.
2. **Background Skills** — pick one as a free 4-rank skill. All listed background skills become **class skills** for the character regardless of class chosen.
3. **Home Language** — native dialect (e.g., Common (Aiel), Common (Borderlands)). Character can read/write all known languages.
4. **Bonus Language Choices** — list of languages the character may learn with Int-bonus language slots (1 point per dialect, 2 points for a fully different language).
5. **Equipment** — pick one starting equipment package (free, in addition to class starting gear/money).

### Acquiring More Background Feats Later

- Any background feat available to your background that you don't pick at 1st level remains available at any later feat slot.
- After 1st level, every **2 ranks of Knowledge (local)** unlocks feats from **one additional** background. By 3rd level (4 ranks) you can pick from your own + up to **two** other backgrounds, plus the standard feat list.

### Restrictions

Some backgrounds impose restrictions, listed under their entry:

- **Skill Restriction** — cannot purchase that skill at 1st level (but may buy it later as a normal class/cross-class skill).
- **Weapon Restriction** — character cannot use those weapon types; encounters using restricted weapons grant no XP.
- **Required Skill** — must take this skill, must always have ranks > current level (i.e., maxed).

## Bonus Language Rules

- Int 12+ characters get extra languages: **1 dialect of Common per +1 of Int modifier, or 1 entirely new language per 2 points** of bonus.
- Speaking the right dialect of Common matters for Diplomacy, Gather Information, Innuendo, Read Lips checks (penalty if mismatched).
- **Class-related bonus languages:**
  - *Algai'd'siswai*: hand-sign languages of all clans/societies (the Far Dareis Mai sign-speech is a full language = 2 points; another is a dialect = 1 point).
  - *Nobles*: the **Old Tongue** — smattering = 1 point, mastery = 2 points.

## Humans: Common Traits

- Medium-size, base speed 30 ft.
- Background determines special feats, equipment, and dialect.

## Background Descriptions

### Aiel

- **Lands:** Aiel Waste (the Three-fold Land), east of the Spine of the World. Harsh desert; southern *Termool* is waterless.
- **Personality:** Driven by *ji'e'toh* (honor and obligation). An Aiel must repay any debt of *toh*. Shame is worse than injury — most Aiel would rather be hurt or killed than suffer shame. They despise wetlanders as weak/foolish.
- **Society:** 12 clans → septs → holds. *Algai'd'siswai* (warrior class) belong to one of 12 warrior societies; society membership is independent of clan.
- **Physical:** Tall, broad-shouldered, fair or red hair, deeply tanned. Warriors and *Far Dareis Mai* cut hair short except for a tail at the nape. Wear *cadin'sor* (gray/ochre/brown desert garb) and *shoufa* (head wrap, veiled when ready to fight/kill).
- **Relations:** Hostile to most non-Aiel, especially Cairhienin (cut down *Avendoraldera*). Tolerate Tuatha'an and gleemen.
- **Language:** Common (Aiel) — laced with Old Tongue. Plus clan + society hand-speech.
- **Adventurers:** Rare; usually responding to clan chief / Wise One orders or prophecy.

### Atha'an Miere (Sea Folk)

- **Lands:** Archipelago south of the Sea of Storms, including the Isles of the Sea Folk; Amayar mainlanders make porcelain there.
- **Personality:** Honest hard bargainers; intensely disciplined under stress; sociable in port (food, music, songs, games).
- **Society:** Clans led by **Wavemistresses**; one is **Mistress of the Ships** ("Queen"). **Sailmistresses** captain ships, with **Windfinder** (channeler navigator) and **Cargomaster** (male, handles trade/defense). Salt-naming custom: women keep mother's surname, gain a "salt name" (e.g., "Running Wave") later.
- **Physical:** Dark wood / chocolate skin tone; barechested under sail (women in loose white blouses on land). Pierced left side of nose with chain to left ear; gold preferred over silver. Barefoot on ship.
- **Relations:** Trade willingly except with Aes Sedai (rarely allowed aboard). Secretive about homeland.
- **Language:** Common (Atha'an Miere).
- **Adventurers:** Rare — leaving ships and islands behind is a hardship.

### Borderlander

- **Lands:** Saldaea, Kandor, Arafel, Shienar — kingdoms along the Blight. Malkier fell a generation ago. Watchtowers along the Blight border use mirrors and fires for fast warning.
- **Personality:** Hard-bitten warriors; "Peace, Beauty, Life" sums up their philosophy. Value beauty all the more for living near the Blight.
- **Physical (varies):** Shienarans tall, dark hair tied in topknot with shaved head, brigandine + two longswords. Arafellins fair, two long braids with silver bells, two longswords on back in cross. Kandori shorter than Shienarans; men keep neat forked beards with silver chains marking guild rank. Saldaeans have tilted almond eyes; light armor; sa'sara dance.
- **Relations:** Among themselves: cordial, tease but unite. View southerners as soft; southerners often see them as harsh.
- **Language:** Common (Borderlands).
- **Adventurers:** Many. Trained from childhood; horsemanship a point of pride.

### Cairhienin

- **Lands:** Ruled lands from Shienar to Haddon Mirk in past glory; now reduced. Capital is Cairhien (Topless Towers). Jangai Pass leads to the Aiel Waste. Fertile lands now lying fallow due to refugee crowding.
- **Personality:** Crave order and control. Obsessive planners. Famous for *Daes Dae'mar* — "Game of Houses" — political scheming. Spreading to Tear, Illian; not yet adopted in Andor.
- **Physical:** Fair-skinned, dark hair/eyes, shorter than midlanders. Nobles wear silks of dark colors with horizontal slashes denoting rank. Noblewomen's hair styled in elaborate towers; broad hoop skirts.
- **Relations:** Mixed with Tear, Andor (rivals/trade). Loathed by Aiel for cutting *Avendoraldera*. Aiel deeply scorn them.
- **Language:** Common (Cairhien).
- **Adventurers:** Often nobles seeking prestige, or commoners escaping the Game.

### Domani

- **Lands:** Arad Doman — northwestern coast, between the Mountains of Mist and Aryth Ocean. Disputes Almoth Plain with Tarabon.
- **Personality:** Hedonistic; love trade, politics, pleasure, falconry, baths, lovers. Cunning, manipulative bargainers. Domani women legendary for seduction (taught the "107 kisses, 93 ways to touch" arts); men known for ferocious tempers.
- **Physical:** Average height; coppery skin tone (some), wide skin/hair color range; hair often dyed. Women's clothing skin-tight, sheer/cut to suggest nudity without exposing — scandalous in most westlands but normal here.
- **Relations:** Welcome foreigners; warring with Tarabon over Almoth Plain.
- **Language:** Common (Arad Doman).
- **Adventurers:** Rare — most Domani find adventuring "hot, sweaty, backbreaking."

### Ebou Dari

- **Lands:** Capital of Altara — chaotic canal-filled trading city at the mouth of the River Eldar. Altara itself is decentralized (Queen rules the city only). Eastern Rahad slum sees street duels hourly.
- **Personality:** Polite, friendly, easygoing — but elaborate code of manners. Will challenge any insult to a duel. Any offended person can challenge; commoner can challenge noble (rarely succeeds). Women's marriage knives (gift from husbands at wedding, "in case he displeases her") record marital history. Ebou Dari law presumes any woman justified in killing a man unless proven otherwise. Scars are beauty marks.
- **Physical:** Olive complexion, brown/black hair, dark eyes; shorter than other westlanders. Women in skirts raised over one knee (commoner) or both (noble); curved daggers in belt or sash.
- **Language:** Common (Ebou Dar).
- **Adventurers:** Often armsmen/woodsmen; large wilder population in Altara provides channelers avoiding the White Tower.

### Illianer

- **Lands:** Sea of Storms coast; capital Illian. Three ruling bodies: King, Council of Nine, Assemblage (a rare commoner political body).
- **Personality:** Despise tyranny but enjoy pomp. Expansive accent; men favor distinctive beards leaving upper lip bare. Drove Whitecloaks back during recent war and restored Altara's rulers.
- **Physical:** Above-average height; moderate complexion, often dark hair/eyes. Men wear pants, light silk shirts, calf-length coats with upturned collars. Women wear low-necked dresses, slippered, wide-brimmed hats with scarves.
- **Relations:** Major trade nation; close to Murandy/Altara. Frigid relations with Tear (no trade); not friendly with Amadicia.
- **Language:** Common (Illian) — thick accent, hardest for outsiders to understand.
- **Adventurers:** Nobles, armsmen, wanderers, wilders, initiates — including Hunters of the Horn (just launched from Illian).

### Midlander

- **Lands:** Andor, Murandy, Far Madding, Ghealdan, Two Rivers, Emond's Field, Baerlon, parts of Amadicia, northern Altara, parts of Illian and Tear.
- **Personality:** Solid, dependable, adaptable, hard-working. Slow to anger. Some carry the ancient blood of Manetheren (a sheen of nobility even in lowest-born peasants). Conservative tastes — dislike ostentation.
- **Physical:** Average height/build; dark hair/eyes typical, fair seen too. Practical clothing; women's dresses to ankles; wools common.
- **Relations:** Center of westlands trade. Welcomed throughout most kingdoms. Murandians distrust outsiders, especially Andorans.
- **Language:** Common (Midlands).
- **Adventurers:** Devoted to the Light; not driven by glory but by doing right.

### Tairen

- **Lands:** Tear — south coast, just west of the Spine of the World. Capital is Tear, guarded by the Stone of Tear. River Erinin port; fertile but lightly populated countryside.
- **Personality:** Self-confident verging on arrogant nobles; rigid noble/commoner divide. **Channeling is forbidden in Tear** (though Aes Sedai are not forbidden). High Lords have hoarded *angreal*, *sa'angreal*, *ter'angreal* in the Stone for generations. Owning a copy of the Prophecies of the Dragon is illegal (Stone of Tear's fall is among them).
- **Physical:** Average height; paler than Ebou Dari, duskier than midlander/Cairhienin. Nobles wear elaborate puffed/padded sleeves; tight breeches. Common men: drab baggy breeches, bright sashes, cloth caps or conical straw hats; often barefoot.
- **Relations:** Despise Illianers. Long designs on Mayene. Aggressive toward Cairhien but trade widely on the Erinin.
- **Language:** Common (Tairen).
- **Adventurers:** Fine armsmen, nobles, wanderers; channelers slipping out to practice safely abroad.

### Tar Valoner

- **Lands:** Island city on the River Erinin at the foot of Dragonmount, between Cairhien and the Borderlands. Home of the **White Tower** (Aes Sedai).
- **Personality:** Practical/midlander-like, but more cosmopolitan, urbane, educated, intensely proud of city. Aes Sedai/novices/Accepted often on the streets — respected, not feared, in Tar Valon itself.
- **Physical:** Average height/build; fair hair/complexions common but variable. Quality fabrics with intricate embroidery preferred.
- **Relations:** Diplomatic center of the world. Most nations respect the Tower; Amadicia and Tear suspicious or hostile.
- **Language:** Common (Midlands) — Tar Valoners speak the midlands dialect.
- **Adventurers:** Worldly attitude prepares many for adventure; Aes Sedai send many out on Tower business.

### Taraboner

- **Lands:** Southwest corner of westlands, between the Mountains of Mist, Aryth Ocean, and the Almoth Plain. Capital Tanchico (on three peninsulas) is one of the largest, oldest cities of the westlands. Hot, humid lowlands.
- **Personality:** Define themselves by **veiled faces** — exposing one's face in private is impolite (even when eating/drinking). Veils thin/heavy/gauzy; full masks for greater privacy. Festivals revolve around masks. Proud of heritage tracing back to Age of Legends. Reputation for hedonism (less than Domani) — gladiatorial circles and entertainments. Complex politics: King + Panarch + Assembly of Lords.
- **Physical:** Average height, somewhat bulky build. Darker than Domani/midlanders, lighter than Ebou Dari. Men: cylindrical felt/leather caps, white baggy trousers and shirts with embroidered panels, bushy mustaches. Women: thin dresses cut to accentuate figure.
- **Relations:** Get along with everyone except Domani (300-year Almoth Plain feud). Tanchico is a major trade harbor.
- **Language:** Common (Tarabon).
- **Adventurers:** Strong-willed, versatile, quick-thinking — merchant wanderers, armsmen, channelers.

### Ogier

Ogier are a **separate species** — about 8–10 feet tall, broad shoulders, broad noses, bushy eyebrows, long tufted ears that twitch with emotion. Lifespan ~5× human. Built many of the westlands' greatest cities (stonemasons).

- **Personality:** Slow, deliberate, careful. Talk things over at length. Never break promises ("Ogier's oath"). Polite to a fault.
- **Society:** Reside in *stedding* — small hidden enclaves (forty-one occupied stedding extant). Led by Councils of Elders, dominated by females. Mothers arrange sons' marriages, often without telling the groom. Ogier women decorate themselves with floral-themed jewelry but never earrings.
- *Stedding* properties:
  - One Power **cannot** be channeled or sensed inside.
  - *Tel'aran'rhiod* cannot be entered from inside.
  - Trollocs/Myrddraal will not enter unless forced.
  - Origins predate the Breaking; some unoccupied stedding still retain mystic properties.
- **The Longing:** An Ogier away from *stedding* too long sickens. Mechanically: must spend at least **one week per year inside a *stedding***, or **lose 1 Constitution per month** away. Recovery: 1 lost Con per day spent inside *stedding*. **Con 0 = death.**
- **Lands:** *Stedding* throughout the westlands, mountainous/hilly regions preferred. Examples: Stedding Tsofu (Cairhien), Stedding Shangtai (Spine), Stedding Jongai (Saldaea), Stedding Chinden (Mountains of Mist).
- **Language:** Ogier own language (kept secret from humans), most also speak Common and/or the Old Tongue.
- **Adventurers:** Rare. Driven by curiosity; in human lands they often act more hastily than other Ogier.

#### Ogier Background Traits

Ogier do **not** receive normal human background benefits. Instead:

- **Ability:** +4 Strength, –4 Dexterity.
- **Size:** Large (10-ft natural reach; –1 size modifier to Defense; easier to hit).
- **Speed:** 40 ft base.
- **Vision:** Low-light vision (twice as far as humans in moonlight/torchlight/etc.).
- **Free Skill:** 4 ranks in one Knowledge or Profession skill, or in Decipher Script. That skill becomes a class skill.
  - Common Knowledge skills: arcana, architecture and engineering, geography, history, local, nature.
  - Common Professions: architect, engineer, gardener, stonemason.
- **Saves:** +2 background bonus on Fortitude saves.
- **Skills:** +2 background bonus on Listen checks (large ears); +2 on Craft checks for stone or building work.
- **Skill/Feat restrictions at character creation:**
  - Cannot purchase ranks in Ride at character creation.
  - Cannot take Armor Proficiency (light/medium/heavy), Exotic or Martial Weapon Proficiency, or any weapon-proficiency feats other than simple weapons at creation. (May be earned later through class advancement.)
- **Channeling:** Ogier **cannot** channel. They cannot take any channeling-class levels, ever.
- **Bonus background feat slot:** May choose Artistic, Education, Living History, or Smooth Talk whenever they have a normal feat slot.
- **Languages:** Home Language Ogier + Common (choose dialect — note: not all Ogier speak Common, but adventuring ones do). Bonus: any Common dialect or Old Tongue.

## Implementation Notes for WheelMUD

Suggested data shape:

```go
type Background struct {
    ID                 string
    Name               string
    BackgroundFeats    []string  // feat IDs available at 1st level
    BackgroundSkills   []string  // pick one at 4 ranks; all become class skills
    HomeLanguage       string
    BonusLanguages     []string
    EquipmentPackages  [][]string // each inner slice is one mutually-exclusive package
    SkillRestriction   string     // e.g., "Ride" for Aiel
    WeaponRestriction  string     // e.g., "sword" for Aiel
    RequiredSkill      string     // e.g., "Profession (sailor)" for Atha'an Miere
}

type Race string // Human, Ogier
type Character struct {
    Race           Race
    BackgroundID   string
    BackgroundFeat string  // chosen at level 1
    BackgroundSkill string // 4 free ranks, also class-skill
}
```

Key invariants and engine rules:

1. **One background per character** — enforce on character creation; no swapping.
2. **Background skills become class skills** for that character regardless of class chosen — store this as a per-character class-skill override list.
3. **Feat list expansion via Knowledge (local):** at character level ≥ 2 and Knowledge (local) ranks ≥ 2*N, allow background-feat selection from N additional backgrounds (cap at 2 per RAW examples).
4. **Required Skill (Atha'an Miere – Profession (sailor)):** ranks must always exceed character level; level-up blocks if not maxed.
5. **Skill Restriction (Aiel – Ride):** disallow purchasing ranks at 1st level only; allow at later levels.
6. **Weapon Restriction (Aiel – sword):** flag character to receive 0 XP from encounters where they used the restricted weapon (will require XP-tracking-with-restriction logic).
7. **Bonus languages:** charge 1 point per dialect of Common, 2 points per fully different language. Int bonus = total points pool; class-related languages (algai'd'siswai, noble) extend the available list.
8. **Ogier is a different race**, not a 12th human background — many human-background mechanics don't apply.
9. **Ogier Longing:** scheduled tick — every month outside a *stedding* applies –1 Con; every day inside restores +1 (capped at original); Con 0 ⇒ death. Stedding rooms must be tagged with an `is_stedding` flag (also gates channeling — see #10).
10. **Stedding flag on rooms** must:
    - Suppress all channeling attempts inside.
    - Block *Tel'aran'rhiod* entry.
    - Reject Trollocs/Myrddraal NPCs from entering except via scripted force.
11. **Ogier creation restrictions:** block selecting Ride, armor proficiency feats, and martial/exotic weapon-prof feats at level 1. Allow later via class.
12. **Channeling forbidden in Tear** (Tairen background lore, but more relevantly a regional law) — separate from *stedding* mechanics; this is a legal/social rule, not a physical block. Probably modeled as guard NPC behavior + crime system, not as a hard `cannot channel` flag.
13. **Background feats** like Shadowspawn Hunter scale (taking multiple times unlocks new creature categories) — make feat instances stackable with progression metadata.

### Data Catalog

Persist all 11 human backgrounds + 1 Ogier "background-equivalent" entry as YAML or DB seed under `internal/world` (mirroring the existing room/exit loader pattern). Each entry should match the `Background` shape above plus narrative flavor (Personality, Physical Description, Relations, Lands, Adventurers) for `look`/`help` text.

### Open Questions

- Does the engine distinguish dialects of Common from fully separate languages, or just track string IDs and rely on the language list to know costs?
- Is the Aiel weapon-restriction XP rule enforced strictly (no XP if a single hit landed with a sword) or per-encounter-primary-weapon? Pick a clear rule before building.
- The Ogier Longing decay rate (1 Con/month) is slow; persistent timers across MUD downtime need a wall-clock-based ledger, not a tick counter.
- The Cosmopolitan feat changes which skills are class-skills — the class-skill table must accept per-feat overrides, not be class-fixed.
