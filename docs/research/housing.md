# Achaea — Player Housing

Source: https://www.achaea.com/game-features/housing/
Fetched: 2026-06-09
Last Updated by site: June 1, 2026

---

## Overview

Achaea housing lets you build a custom home one room at a time. You write the title and
description of every room yourself, place exits, hide them, add doors, and furnish with
goods crafted by other players.

This is closer to building a small zone than decorating an apartment.

---

## Building

- Start with one room; add rooms one at a time as long as you can supply credits
- **Full control per room**: write room title and description, place exits, hide exits,
  add doors
- Connect rooms in any layout — including secret passages, hidden doors, war rooms
- Most homes sit in a city subdivision (ties character to a place and politics)

---

## Cost

### Room Credits

- Each room: 10 room credits
- Each door: 1 credit

Credits are bought at the **Property Office in Delos** and cost:
- 200 wood + 100 stone per credit (paid in commodities, not gold)
- Commodities come from the player economy — gather your own or buy them
- A large home represents real commodity spend

### Additional Gold Costs (per modification)

| Item | Gold Cost |
|------|-----------|
| Room title | 10,000 gp |
| Custom room description | 10,000 gp |
| New exit | 1,000 gp |
| Hidden exit | 50,000 gp |

No level gates — if you can fund it, you can build it.

---

## Furnishing

- Furniture made by other players via the **Furnishing tradeskill**
- Items are player-designed and player-crafted, not game-generated
- Types: chairs, tables, benches, thrones, desks, dressers, etc.
- Buy or commission from crafters
- A well-furnished home showcases player craftsmanship

---

## Servants

Hire servants and place them in your home. Types available:
- Butler
- Maid
- Cook
- Steward
- Page

### Customization

- Set appearance, name, and description
- Customize reactions to events in the house
- Set wandering vs. stationary behavior
- No upkeep cost once hired

### Limitations

- Servants don't take direct commands
- Don't execute scripted routines
- But turn an empty building into something that feels inhabited

---

## Roleplay Value

- A home is yours in a way few games allow
- Descriptions you write tell a visitor who your character is before you say a word
- A spymaster's home reads very differently from a scholar's
- Where you build (which city) roots your character in place and politics

---

## Design Notes

- Cost paid in **commodities, not gold** — directly links housing to the player economy
  (players need mines, crafters, traders to build a home)
- No level gating — entirely funded, not gated by progression
- Hidden exits at 50,000 gold create a meaningful investment for security/mystery
- Servant system adds flavor without requiring NPC scripting infrastructure
