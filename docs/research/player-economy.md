# Achaea — Player Economy: Mining, Shops, Trade

Source: https://www.achaea.com/game-features/player-economy/
Fetched: 2026-06-09
Last Updated by site: June 1, 2026

---

## Overview

The Achaea economy is fully player-run. Players mine minerals, brew curatives, distill
venoms, craft gear, set the prices, and run the shops that sell it all. No fixed NPC prices
on key goods — supply and demand genuinely move based on player activity.

---

## Mining

### How It Works

1. Prospect for a mineral lode
2. Build a mine on the lode
3. Deploy squads (Legions) that extract raw commodities automatically
4. Store commodities (they decay if improperly stored)
5. Pay daily upkeep for the mine and each miner

Run poorly → lose coin. Run well → steady profit from commodities other players need.

### Legions

Mines run on Legions — squads you command for work and defense.

- **Defend**: Deploy soldier squads to protect your mine
- **Attack**: Send Legions to assault rival players' mines and strongholds
- Capturing rivals' mines is possible — holding a productive mine means defending it

This creates PvP-adjacent competition even in the "economic" path.

### Output

Raw commodities feed into the crafting system. Crafters need raw materials; many goods
depend on a steady supply from mines.

---

## Player Shops

- Located in player cities — each city has a limited number of shops
- **Scarcity is deliberate**: limited slots = shops are valuable, holding one is status
- Usually acquired by taking over from another player, not opening fresh
- Owner stocks wares and sets all prices themselves
- Can sell: crafted goods, curatives, venoms, commodities, exotic treasures
- Combat players are constant buyers of curatives and venoms → steady demand

### Shop + City Relationship

Running a shop ties you into the life of a player city. You compete with other shopkeepers
for the same buyers. A well-run shop in a busy city becomes a genuine asset and a name
people remember.

---

## Importation and Commodity Trading

- **Importation**: source goods where cheap/plentiful, move them where wanted
- **Delos commodity market**: central exchange where individuals and cities buy and sell raw
  materials
- Price arbitrage between cities/regions is a real trading strategy
- Pairs well with shopkeeping (shop = fixed place to sell what you import)
- Same instincts carry into seafaring (ships move goods between distant ports)

---

## Getting Started

Recommended order:
1. Learn a tradeskill first (teaches you market value of items)
2. Watch prices before producing (note what's expensive, scarce, climbs during conflict)
3. Open a shop or trade commodities (small storefront or a few trades)
4. Scale into mining or larger operation (once you understand the market)

---

## Design Notes

- "The players who pay attention to the economy tend to end up the richest in the game"
- Economy rewards attention and good decisions, not just combat
- Multiple viable paths: mining, shopkeeping, crafting, importation, commodity arbitrage
- Can make money without fighting at all
- Legions system creates conflict even on the economic track — mines can be raided
