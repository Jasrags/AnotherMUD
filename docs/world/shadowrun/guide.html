Player's Guide

A traveler's orientation, drawn from the world itself.

Getting Started

You begin in Westlake Plaza, in Downtown Seattle.

Ferrocrete and polished granite open into the plaza at the centre of Downtown, ringed by arcologies whose logos turn slow against the drizzle. A dead fountain holds a skin of rainwater and cigarette ends, and grav-ads drift overhead murmuring corp slogans to no one. Office drones cut across in a hurry, eyes on their commlinks. At the plaza's edge, where the grav-ads' glare thins to shadow, a fixer in a long coat leans over a crate of wares — the sprawl's quiet commerce running on under the corporate gaze. The corporate canyon of Fifth Avenue runs north, the neon of Pike Street glows south, historic Pioneer Square lies east, and the cold air off Elliott Bay drifts in from the west.

Paths lead north to Fifth Avenue, south to Pike Street, east to Pioneer Square, west to The Elliott Bay Waterfront, up to The Flop, down to The Maintenance Sublevel.

The World

Unassigned region

Bellevue — Across Lake Washington from Downtown, Bellevue is the other side of the wall from Redmond — walled, manicured, gated, and wealthy. Corp families live behind SIN-checked gates and private armies; the malls are cathedrals and the lawns are drone-patrolled. To a runner it is a place you infiltrate, not a place you belong: AA security, fast response, and no tolerance for the SINless.

  • Stuffer Shack shop
  • Stuffer Shack — Back Kitchen shop quest giver

Downtown Seattle — The corporate heart of the metroplex: mirrored arcologies and government spires crowd a skyline that never fully darkens, while the streets below run on upscale nightlife, Knight Errant patrols, and the quiet trade of the shadows. This is the first authored district of the Seattle build — the Downtown core the gazetteer says to raise first (sr2075_geography_mud.md, Build order), radiating out to the barrens and corp enclaves later.

  • Dante's Inferno shop quest giver
  • The Big Rhino quest giver
  • Westlake Plaza shop

Downtown Sublevel — The dead maintenance warren beneath Westlake — service tunnels, transformer vaults, and transit stubs the corp council walled off and forgot. No lighting, no patrols, no reason to be here unless you're moving something that shouldn't be seen. A pocket of true darkness under the neon.

Glow City — Southeastern Redmond's radioactive heart: the dead reactor zone the Barrens call the Glow. Nothing living belongs here — the rubble ticks on a Geiger counter, the light bleeds sickly green off every surface, and an unshielded runner measures their stay in minutes. Only the desperate, the mutated, and the suited come this deep. The reward for surviving it is whatever the old world left glowing in the ruins.

Renton — South of the lake and east of the interstate, Renton is where the sprawl does its dirty work: warehouses, rail yards, and small manufacturing under a permanent grey drizzle, a working-class district that has definitely seen better days. It runs more gangs than anywhere but the Redmond Barrens, and it is Humanis Policlub country — the anti-metahuman club keeps its Seattle hall here, and the sentiment on the street matches. B-security means the patrols are thin and slow; the Mafia and the Yakuza both work the freight, and a runner who keeps their head down can find cheap chrome and cheaper trouble in equal measure.

  • Greasy Ben's shop

The ACHE — The Arcology Commercial and Housing Enclave — the mirrored ziggurat that was the Renraku Arcology before 2059, before the AI sealed a hundred thousand people inside and only some walked back out. Reclaimed, renamed, re-leased: forty-odd floors of shopping concourse, wageslave housing, and corporate suites stacked into a self-contained city that never has to touch the street. The blast doors to the sealed sublevels are still welded shut. (docs/specs/transit.md — the elevator slice; the ACHE is the sr2075 gazetteer's Downtown arcology landmark.)

  • ACHE — Commercial Concourse shop

The Ash Flats — West of Puyallup City the ground gives out to Rainier's ash barrens — grey drifts over venting sulphur, the air a slow poison, the light strangled to a permanent dusk. The corrupt local machine writes it off; the smugglers cut through it because nobody else will follow. Toxic shapes are said to walk the deeper flats, but the fumes alone will kill an unsealed runner long before anything with teeth finds them.

The Deep Freeze — A cryogenic cold-storage facility off the Redmond strip, breached and abandoned when the coolant lines blew. What the corp kept frozen here — organs, bodies, colder secrets — is still frozen, and so is anyone who lingers too long without a thermal suit. The cold counterpart to the Glow: not radiation but a killing chill, and the same rule — come sealed, or don't come at all.

The Downtown Metro — The Metroplex Transit Authority's underground line beneath Downtown — a single loop of tiled platforms and ozone-smelling tunnels that shuttles wageslaves and runners alike between the corporate canyon, the Pike strip, and the waterfront. The trains run themselves on a fixed schedule; you catch one when it's in. (docs/specs/transit.md — the scheduled-policy subway; the same conveyance machine as the ACHE elevator, running a timetable instead of taking calls.)

The Ork Underground — Beneath Pioneer Square the ork and troll community that was driven off the surface built a city of its own — a warren of old Seattle's buried streets, cisterns, and service tunnels, lit by strung work-lamps and warmed by its own crowded life. It has an economy, a culture, and a government the surface barely acknowledges, and it is growing more organized and more assertive every year. The law does not come down here; the Skraacha do, an ork gang turned community defenders who keep the inhabited quarters safe and the deep tunnels to themselves. A surprisingly stable mana flow runs through the rock — the spiritual life of a people who made a home out of the dark.

  • The Skraacha Hall quest giver
  • The Underground Market shop

The Puyallup Barrens — The metroplex's other great Barrens, sprawled south over volcanic ground with Mount Rainier looming on the skyline and ash fields smoking near its feet. Burned through in gang wars, run by a corrupt local machine, and threaded with smuggling routes — Puyallup is where the Sixth World's cargo moves when nobody wants a manifest. Z-security at heart, a nominal C at its calmest.

  • The Chrome Den shop
  • Loveland shop
  • Puyallup City shop

The Redmond Barrens — Northeast of the metroplex, past the Bellevue wall, the grid simply stops: the Redmond Barrens, three-quarters SINless, all gang turf and urban decay, with the radioactive scar of Glow City festering in its southeast. Law doesn't come here — Knight Errant writes it off and lets the gangs sort it out. The classic shadowrunner's backyard: cheap gear, cheaper lives, and nobody asking questions.

  • Downfall shop
  • Hollywood quest giver
  • The Avondale Chop-Shop quest giver
  • Touristville shop

The Safehouse — A fixer's flop tucked above a Downtown alley — three rooms of salvaged furniture, a gun table, and a back-room chop-doc, where a runner too green to know better gets staked, geared, and pointed at the sprawl. Safe, quiet, and the last easy place you'll stand before Seattle starts charging you for the air.

  • The Back Room shop
  • The Gun Table shop

The Seattle Sprawl — The Emerald City, drowned in rain and neon. Corporate arcologies wall off the skyline while the streets below run on grey markets, gang turf, and whatever the shadows will pay for. SR-M3c-1 opens a single corner of it — the district proper arrives with the runners who work it.

  • A Rain-Slick Street Corner shop
