Player's Guide

A traveler's orientation, drawn from the world itself.

Getting Started

You begin in The Green, in Emond's Field (Two Rivers).

The Green is the heart of Emond's Field, a broad common of trampled grass where the village lanes all run together. The Winespring Inn stands close on the east side, whitewashed and red-roofed on its low stone foundation, and to the south the ground dips to where the Winespring rises. West, the Quarry Road runs out past the smithy toward the distant trees and the grey shoulders of the Mountains of Mist; north, the road climbs away toward Watch Hill. It is a settled, unhurried place, a long way from anywhere a map would trouble to name.

Paths lead north to The North Road, south to The Winespring, east to The Winespring Inn — Common Room, west to The Quarry Road.

The World

Andor

Arien — A small waystop on the Caemlyn Road between Whitebridge and Four Kings, no more than an inn, a wainwright's yard, and a scatter of houses where the highway pauses for breath. Arien lives off the wagon trade that never stops rolling through it — a place to change a wheel, water a team, and sleep a night under a roof — and asks nothing of a traveller but his custom and that he move along in the morning.

  • Arien — the Wagoner's Rest shop
  • Arien — the Wainwright's Yard shop

Baerlon — A large walled town in the far west of Andor, set in the foothills of the Mountains of Mist and grown fat on the iron and silver the miners dig out of the peaks above. Within its wooden wall the streets are crowded and close, loud with the trade of half a dozen roads that meet here, and watched — by the Queen's men, by the merchants' guards, and lately by the white-cloaked Children of the Light, who have taken to walking the streets as though they owned them. To Two Rivers eyes it is a city; to the rest of Andor it is a rough mining town a long way from anywhere.

  • Baerlon — the Market Square shop
  • Baerlon — the Silverworks shop trainer
  • Baerlon — the Stag and Lion shop

Caemlyn — The capital of Andor and the fairest city in the west of the world — a great ring of grey wall enclosing the bustling New City of red brick and shining slate, and within that, raised on its own hill behind a second wall, the ancient Inner City: white-towered, garden-hung, its silver-streaked stone laid by Ogier hands in the first days after the Breaking and unmatched by anything men have built since. Here the Queen of Andor keeps her court in the Royal Palace, the roads of half the realm meet, and the trade and rumour and splendour of a great kingdom all come together under the rising-sun banner.

  • Caemlyn — the New City shop
  • Caemlyn — the Queen's Blessing shop
  • Caemlyn — the Royal Palace quest giver

Carysford — A pretty river-village on the Caemlyn Road where it crosses the little River Cary by an old stone ford, the last quiet stop before the road runs on to the capital. Willows trail in the slow green water, a watermill turns on the far bank, and the village makes its modest living off the river-trade and the travellers who pause to water their teams and take a meal in sight of Caemlyn's distance. It is a settled, easy place, proud of its ford and its mill and little troubled by the wide world that passes through it.

  • Carysford — the Leaping Fish shop

Comfrey — A small, hardy upland settlement tucked into a sheltered fold of the Mountains of Mist north-west of Baerlon, on the very edge of the settled world. Comfrey lives off the high pasture, a little mining, and the trickle of trade that passes on the back-country road; its folk are weathered and close-mouthed and used to the mist and the cold, and they reckon anyone from below the foothills a soft outlander. Beyond it the Mountains of Mist climb on into cloud and silence.

  • Comfrey — the Miner's Rest shop

Four Kings — A grubby, mean-spirited town in the middle of Andor, grown up where the rough roads from the southern mines cross the Caemlyn Road and existing for no reason but the trade that passes through it. Four Kings lives off the merchant trains — wagon-yards, cheap inns, cheaper liquor, and a transient population of teamsters, peddlers, and folk who would rather not be asked their business. There is no wall, no Queen's Guard worth the name, and little law a hard coin cannot bend; a careful traveller keeps a hand on his purse and his back to a wall, and does not linger after dark.

  • Four Kings — the Dancing Cartman shop
  • Four Kings — the Wagon Yard shop

Market Sheran — A prosperous farming village on the Caemlyn Road east of Four Kings, as honest and settled as Four Kings is mean — a tidy place of stone-and-thatch houses about a proper green, a market that gives the village its name, and well-tended fields running out to every horizon. The folk are Andoran farmers, hard-working and close with a coin and a touch wary of strangers in uncertain times, but the Queen's peace holds here and a civil traveller is met civilly enough.

  • Market Sheran — the Harvest Home shop
  • Market Sheran — the Market shop

The Baerlon Road — North of the Taren the easy green of the Two Rivers gives way at once to Andor proper: a harder, plainer country of stony pasture and wind-bent hedgerow, climbing all the while toward the grey wall of the Mountains of Mist. The road that runs through it is an Andoran road — wider, older, and busier than anything south of the river — bound for the mining town of Baerlon under the peaks. This is the first stretch of the wide world beyond home, and it does not pretend to be friendly.

The Caemlyn Road — The great east-west spine of Andor, running clear across the kingdom from the White Bridge over the Arinelle to the gates of Caemlyn itself. Here at its western end it carries the trade of half the realm — merchant trains, ore wagons down from Baerlon, drovers and peddlers and the Queen's couriers — over open, rolling farmland that falls away gradually toward the river. After the thin pasture of the high country it feels prosperous and busy and safe, a real road in a settled land.

Whitebridge — A bustling river town on the eastern bank of the Arinelle, grown up where the Caemlyn Road crosses the great White Bridge that gives the place its name. It is a town of trade and travellers — the last sizeable stop before the long road east to Caemlyn, a landing for the river craft that work down to Illian, and a crossroads where the news and the rumour of half of Andor change hands over ale and bargains. The Queen's law holds here, after a fashion, and the wonder of the bridge draws a stare from every newcomer who has never seen the like.

  • Whitebridge — the Wayfarers' Rest shop
  • Whitebridge — the Market Square shop

Two Rivers

Deven Ride — The southernmost of the Two Rivers villages, a quiet scatter of farms and sheepfolds smaller even than Watch Hill. Deven Ride keeps to itself, raises good wool and better mutton, and is content to let the rest of the world go by on the far side of the hedges.

Emond's Field — A farming village in the Two Rivers, the far-western corner of Andor — so far from any throne that the folk here govern themselves, keep their own Mayor and Village Council, and barely think of themselves as Andoran at all.

  • The Winespring Inn — Common Room recruiter
  • The Winespring Inn — Kitchen shop trainer
  • The al'Thor Farm stable
  • The Smithy shop trainer

Stedding Chinden — A hidden Ogier stedding folded into a high sheltered vale of the Mountains of Mist — the nearest of the forty-one occupied stedding to the Two Rivers, though few in Emond's Field have ever seen it or know the way. Within its bounds the One Power lies still and unreachable, no Shadowspawn will cross the threshold, and the great trees are sung rather than felled. The Ogier keep to themselves here, slow and gentle and long of memory, tending gardens older than the nations of men.

Taren Ferry — The northernmost settlement of the Two Rivers, clinging to the south bank of the River Taren where the only road out of the district meets the only crossing — a rope-and-barge ferry worked by the Ferry family. The rest of the Two Rivers holds Taren Ferry folk a little sharp, a little too fond of a bargain, and not quite to be trusted; the ferry folk return the compliment.

The Mountains of Mist — The grey western wall of the Two Rivers, its lower slopes broken into stony sand-hills where the Westwood gives out. Old diggings cut into the rock here, worked for iron in years long past and worth working still by anyone with a pick and the patience to swing it.

The Waterwood — The low, wet country along the eastern edge of the Two Rivers, where the land loses its nerve and dissolves into fen — a maze of reed-beds, black standing pools, and drowned trees that the folk of Deven Ride call the Mire and stay well clear of. The White River loses itself somewhere in the heart of it. The Waterwood gives fenroot and reed and a living to the handful of trappers bold enough to work it, but it takes the unwary just as readily: the footing lies, the leeches are patient, and there are older, quieter things in the deep water that the Wisdom would rather you did not go looking for.

The Westwood — The forest west of Emond's Field, climbing from the village fields toward the feet of the Mountains of Mist. Oak and leatherleaf give way to pine the higher it goes; folk gather wood and herbs at its edges and tell their children not to wander too deep.

Watch Hill — The northern village of the Two Rivers, set on a low rise that looks out over the surrounding fields — high enough that a watcher on its crown can see trouble coming a long way off, which in the bad years has mattered more than once. Smaller than Emond's Field, and prouder of its view.
